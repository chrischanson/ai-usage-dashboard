**Dates:** May 1–3, 2026 (Subscription Concert 8 - Finale)  
**Conductor:** John DeMain  
**Featured Soloists:**  
*   **Alessio Bax** (Piano, MSO Debut)  
*   **John Holiday** (Countertenor)  
*   **Alexandra LoBianco** (Soprano)

**Venue:** Overture Hall, Madison, WI  

---

## 📖 Concert Background & Context
"Voices Eternal" served as the final subscription concert of the Madison Symphony Orchestra’s historic 100th anniversary season and the final subscription series conducted by John DeMain as Music Director, marking the end of his illustrious 32-year leadership. 

The program was curated by Maestro DeMain to serve as a retrospective of the orchestra’s history and his own career:
*   **Wagner’s Prelude to *Die Meistersinger*:** A direct nod to the orchestra's history, as this piece was performed by the amateur Madison Civic Symphony during its inaugural 1926 season.
*   **MacDowell’s Piano Concerto No. 2:** Featured the Madison debut of world-renowned pianist Alessio Bax.
*   **Bernstein’s *Chichester Psalms*:** A sentimental reprise of the first choral work John DeMain conducted with the MSO in 1994, featuring acclaimed countertenor John Holiday.
*   **Bruckner’s *Te Deum*:** Selected by DeMain to conclude his final subscription series, providing a massive, celebratory, and triumphant climax to the MSO's centennial year.

*Note: The concert was originally announced to feature the world premiere of Jake Heggie's "EARTH: A Choral Symphony" and Beethoven's 5th, but the program was revised in early 2026 to this historical retrospective program.*

## ✍️ Critical Review & Audience Reception
Local writeups and reviews from Madison critics (including reflections in *The Cap Times* and *Isthmus*) described the weekend as a deeply emotional, monumental triumph:

*   **Wagner's Prelude:** The orchestra opened the concert with a robust, brilliant performance of the *Die Meistersinger* Prelude, showing rich, unified brass and string playing that immediately set a celebratory tone.
*   **MacDowell’s Piano Concerto No. 2:** Pianist Alessio Bax made a sensational debut. Critics praised his romantic expressiveness, dramatic sweep, and technical clarity. He brought both thunderous power to the outer movements and a delicate, singing lyricism to the second movement, establishing an immediate connection with the audience.
*   **Bernstein’s Chichester Psalms:** Countertenor John Holiday’s performance of the Psalm 23 solo was described as "hauntingly beautiful and pure." The chorus handled the complex Hebrew text, syncopated rhythms, and jazz-inflected harmonies of Bernstein’s work with exceptional rhythmic snap and clarity.
*   **Bruckner’s Te Deum:** Bruckner's masterpiece served as a thrilling and massive wall of sound. The vocal soloists (LoBianco, Zabala, Walker, Boehler) were praised for their power and blend, soaring over the massive fortissimo of the orchestra and the hallowed acoustics of Overture Hall. The Madison Symphony Chorus sang with incredible power, unity, and dynamic control.
*   **The Farewell Tribute:** At the conclusion of the concert, the audience erupted into a massive, extended standing ovation. Reviewers described the atmosphere as thick with emotion, with many tearful audience members and musicians. John DeMain was presented with floral tributes and long applause, honoring his 32 years of artistic leadership.
