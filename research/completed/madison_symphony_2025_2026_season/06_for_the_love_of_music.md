# 💖 For the Love of Music: Centennial Festival Symphony Concert

**Date:** June 14, 2026  
**Conductor:** John DeMain  
**Featured Soloists:**  
*   **Julian Rhee** (Violin)  
*   **Alexandra LoBianco** (Soprano)  
*   **Kara Morgan** (Mezzo-Soprano)  
*   **Michael Deshield** (Tenor)  
*   **Craig Irvin** (Baritone)  
*   **Madison Symphony Chorus** (Beverly Taylor, Director)  
**Venue:** Overture Hall, Madison, WI  

---

## 📖 Concert Background & Context
Held on Sunday, June 14, 2026, at 4:30 p.m., this concert served as the grand finale of the Madison Symphony Orchestra's (MSO) Centennial Festival Weekend. The free, unticketed community concert marked the **absolute final performance conducted by John DeMain as Music Director**, concluding his historic 32-year tenure. 

Offered as a "gift to the community" to say thank you for a century of support, the concert drew a capacity crowd that packed Overture Hall, with fans lining up hours in advance. The program featured a selection of classical "greatest hits" and concluded with Beethoven's massive choral statement of universal brotherhood.

---

## 🎼 Repertoire & Program
*   **George Gershwin:** *An American in Paris*
*   **Pablo de Sarasate:** *Carmen Fantasy for Violin and Orchestra, Op. 25* (featuring violinist Julian Rhee)
*   **Pablo de Sarasate:** *Zigeunerweisen, Op. 20* (Encore featuring violinist Julian Rhee with the orchestra)
*   **Jean Sibelius:** *Finlandia, Op. 26*
*   **Gustav Holst:** Selections from *The Planets* (including *Mars*, *Venus*, *Mercury*, and *Jupiter*)
*   **Ludwig van Beethoven:** Choral Finale (Ode to Joy) from *Symphony No. 9 in D minor, Op. 125*

## ✍️ Critical Review & Audience Reception
As John DeMain’s final concert with the MSO, the event was described by attendees and local commentators as an emotionally overwhelming, historic triumph:

*   **An American in Paris:** The concert opened with George Gershwin’s *An American in Paris*, a piece close to John DeMain’s heart. The orchestra performed it with a jazz-inflected swagger and warmth, showing a relaxed, joyful musical communication between DeMain and his players.
*   **Julian Rhee’s Carmen Fantasy & Encore:** Violinist Julian Rhee (a local favorite and previous winner of the Bolz Young Artist Competition) gave a sensational performance of Sarasate’s *Carmen Fantasy*. Critics and observers praised his dazzling, effortless technique, rich tone, and charismatic stage presence. His virtuosic double-stops and lightning-fast runs drew immediate, enthusiastic cheers from the crowd. 
    
    Following the performance, the crowd's thunderous applause coaxed Rhee back onto the stage for a rare treat: an encore performed with the full orchestra—Sarasate's dramatic and fiery *Zigeunerweisen*, which further electrified Overture Hall.
*   **Finlandia & Community Spirit:** The orchestra performed Sibelius’ majestic *Finlandia*. The reading was powerful and stirring, evoking deep themes of national pride and communal resilience, which resonated strongly with the Madison community.
*   **A Journey Through the Cosmos: Selections from *The Planets*:** In the second half, the orchestra also performed *Mars*, *Venus*, *Mercury*, and *Jupiter* from Gustav Holst’s *The Planets*. John DeMain guided the orchestra through the contrasting moods of these movements—from the menacing, pounding 5/4 rhythm of *Mars (The Bringer of War)* and the peaceful, translucent strings of *Venus (The Bringer of Peace)*, to the fleet-footed, shimmering woodwinds of *Mercury (The Winged Messenger)* and the triumphant, broad hymnic tune of *Jupiter (The Bringer of Jollity)*. The interpretation was highly praised for its color, precision, and dynamic range, highlighting the orchestra's formidable brass and percussion sections.
*   **Beethoven’s Ode to Joy & Historical Full Circle:** The concert concluded with the monumental Choral Finale of Beethoven's Ninth Symphony. The MSO Chorus and the four vocal soloists delivered a soaring, triumphant, and unifying performance. The final, explosive prestissimo ended in a burst of joy, eliciting a roaring ten-minute ovation. 
    
    This choice of finale held profound historical significance: at the conclusion of John DeMain's very first season as Music Director in May 1995, he had conducted Beethoven’s Ninth Symphony (along with Symphony No. 1) at the Oscar Mayer Theatre. Bookending his 32-year tenure with the same epic choral masterpiece brought his leadership to a poetic, emotional full circle.
*   **The Farewell & Lobby Celebration:** As the final notes of the Beethoven faded, the audience erupted into a thunderous standing ovation that lasted for over ten minutes. The crowd showered DeMain with applause, cheers, and many tears. Musicians on stage also joined the applause, recognizing DeMain’s transformation of the MSO into a top-tier regional orchestra over his 32 years. 
    
    Following the performance, the celebration spilled out into the Overture Center lobby for a free public reception. The festive atmosphere concluded with live Afro-Cuban and Latin rhythms by the local band **Grupo Candela**, a special dessert bar, and a community Prosecco toast honoring Maestro John DeMain's three decades of musical service.
*   **The Verdict:** Reviewers described the evening as a perfect, deeply emotional, and fitting farewell to one of the most significant figures in Madison's cultural history, leaving a legacy that will shape the MSO for its next century.
