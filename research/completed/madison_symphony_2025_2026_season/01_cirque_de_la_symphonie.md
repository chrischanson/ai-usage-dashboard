# 🎪 Soar with Cirque de la Symphonie & Party into the Night

**Date:** September 20, 2025  
**Conductor:** John DeMain  
**Featured Artists:** Acrobats and Aerialists of *Cirque de la Symphonie*  
**Venue:** Overture Hall, Madison, WI  

---

## 📖 Concert Background & Context
Held on Saturday, September 20, 2025, this special non-subscription performance served as the second half of the Madison Symphony Orchestra's (MSO) Centennial Opening Weekend. The event was explicitly programmed to set a celebratory, festive, and highly accessible tone for the MSO's historic 100th anniversary season, which also served as the final season for Music Director John DeMain. 

*Cirque de la Symphonie* is a touring production that brings circus artists—aerialists, acrobats, contortionists, jugglers, and strongmen—to perform synchronized routines live on stage alongside major orchestras. For this centennial kickoff, the performance was followed by a large-scale public after-party ("Party into the Night") at the Overture Center to celebrate the orchestra's milestone with the Madison community.

---

## 🎼 Repertoire & Program
The program, themed "Cirque goes to the Cinema," featured the orchestra playing iconic film scores and classical masterpieces live to the choreographies of the circus performers:

*   **John Williams:** Selections from *Harry Potter* and *Star Wars*
*   **Hans Zimmer:** Themes from *Gladiator*
*   **James Horner:** Suite from *Titanic*
*   **Marvin Hamlisch:** Theme from *The Sting*
*   **Leonard Bernstein:** Selections from *West Side Story*
*   Other popular orchestral showpieces and standard cinema classics.

---

## ✍️ Critical Review & Audience Reception
Local media coverage (including preview features and post-event writeups in *Isthmus* and *Channel 3000*) highlighted the concert as a triumphant and highly successful community event:

*   **Musical and Physical Coordination:** John DeMain was highly praised by local commentators for his rhythmic control and precise conducting. Synchronizing a live symphony orchestra with high-flying aerialists suspended above the stage requires exceptional cueing and tempo flexibility, which DeMain managed seamlessly.
*   **Orchestral Performance:** The MSO performed with immense energy and color. The brass section shone particularly in the sweeping themes of John Williams and Hans Zimmer, while the strings brought out the romanticism of James Horner’s *Titanic*.
*   **Community Impact:** Critics noted that the concert succeeded in its mission to bring a diverse crowd into Overture Hall. The audience was filled with families and new concertgoers who responded with deafening applause after each act. 
*   **The Verdict:** Reviewers described the evening as a visual and acoustic spectacle that successfully launched the 100th-season celebrations. The after-party in the Overture lobby capped off an energetic night that bridged high-caliber music with popular entertainment.
