# 💖 For the Love of Music: Centennial Festival Symphony Concert

**Date:** June 14, 2026  
**Conductors:** John DeMain & Lori Grapentine (*Finlandia*)  
**Featured Soloists:**  
*   **Julian Rhee** (Violin)  
*   **Alexandra LoBianco** (Soprano)  
*   **Kara Morgan** (Mezzo-Soprano)  
*   **Michael Deshield** (Tenor)  
*   **Craig Irvin** (Baritone)  
*   **Madison Symphony Chorus** (Beverly Taylor, Director)  
**Venue:** Overture Hall, Madison, WI  

---

## 📖 Concert Background & Context
Held on Sunday, June 14, 2026, at 4:30 p.m., this concert served as the grand finale of the Madison Symphony Orchestra's (MSO) Centennial Festival Weekend. The free, unticketed community concert marked the **absolute final performance conducted by John DeMain as Music Director**, concluding his historic 32-year tenure. 

Offered as a "gift to the community" to say thank you for a century of support, the concert drew a capacity crowd that packed Overture Hall, with fans lining up hours in advance. The program featured a selection of classical "greatest hits" and concluded with Beethoven's massive choral statement of universal brotherhood.

---

## 🎼 Repertoire & Program
*   **George Gershwin:** *An American in Paris*
*   **Pablo de Sarasate:** *Carmen Fantasy for Violin and Orchestra, Op. 25* (featuring violinist Julian Rhee)
*   **Jean Sibelius:** *Finlandia, Op. 26* (conducted by guest conductor Lori Grapentine)
*   **Gustav Holst:** Selections from *The Planets*
*   **Ludwig van Beethoven:** Choral Finale (Ode to Joy) from *Symphony No. 9 in D minor, Op. 125*

---

## ✍️ Critical Review & Audience Reception
As John DeMain’s final concert with the MSO, the event was described by attendees and local commentators as an emotionally overwhelming, historic triumph:

*   **A Festive Opening:** The concert opened with Gershwin's *An American in Paris*, a piece close to John DeMain's heart. The orchestra performed it with a jazz-inflected swagger and warmth, showing a relaxed, joyful musical communication between DeMain and his players.
*   **Julian Rhee’s Carmen Fantasy:** Violinist Julian Rhee (a local favorite and previous winner of the Bolz Young Artist Competition) gave a sensational performance of Sarasate’s *Carmen Fantasy*. Critics and observers praised his dazzling, effortless technique, rich tone, and charismatic stage presence. His virtuosic double-stops and lightning-fast runs drew immediate, enthusiastic cheers from the crowd.
*   **Finlandia & Community Leadership:** In a moving gesture, guest conductor Lori Grapentine took the podium to lead Sibelius’ *Finlandia*. The performance was described as powerful and majestic, serving as a reminder of the orchestra's deep community roots and public engagement.
*   **Beethoven’s Ode to Joy:** The concert concluded with the monumental Choral Finale of Beethoven's Ninth Symphony. The MSO Chorus and the four vocal soloists delivered a soaring, triumphant, and unifying performance. The final, explosive prestissimo ended in a burst of joy.
*   **The Farewell:** As the final notes of the Beethoven faded, the audience erupted into a thunderous standing ovation that lasted for over ten minutes. The crowd showered DeMain with applause, cheers, and many tears. Musicians on stage also joined the applause, recognizing DeMain’s transformation of the MSO into a top-tier regional orchestra over his 32 years. 
*   **The Verdict:** Reviewers described the evening as a perfect, deeply emotional, and fitting farewell to one of the most significant figures in Madison's cultural history, leaving a legacy that will shape the MSO for its next century.
