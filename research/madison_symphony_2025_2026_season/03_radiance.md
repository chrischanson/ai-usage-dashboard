# ✨ Radiance

**Dates:** November 21–23, 2025 (Subscription Concert 2)  
**Conductor:** Robert Moody (Guest Conductor)  
**Featured Soloist:** **Alban Gerhardt** (Cello)  
**Venue:** Overture Hall, Madison, WI  

---

## 📖 Concert Background & Context
"Radiance" was the second subscription concert of the Madison Symphony Orchestra's 2025–2026 Centennial Season. This weekend was significant because it featured guest conductor **Robert Moody** (Music Director of the Memphis Symphony Orchestra and Arizona Musicfest), who was appearing as part of the MSO's search process to select a successor for the retiring John DeMain. 

The program was curated around themes of light, structure, and color. It opened with Christopher Theofanidis’ modern masterpiece *Rainbow Body*, a piece built on the chants of medieval mystic Hildegard von Bingen. It was followed by Joseph Haydn’s classical Cello Concerto No. 2, featuring world-renowned cellist Alban Gerhardt. The concert concluded with Maurice Ravel’s grand, colorful orchestration of Modest Mussorgsky’s *Pictures at an Exhibition*.

## ✍️ Critical Review & Audience Reception
Local media writeups and classical music reviews praised guest conductor Robert Moody and the performance's vibrant sonic palette:

*   **Theofanidis’ Rainbow Body:** Critics loved the opening work, describing the MSO's performance as "luminous and shimmering." Moody was praised for his careful control of the work’s dense, evolving brass textures and the organic growth from the quiet opening string theme to the massive orchestral climaxes.
*   **Haydn’s Cello Concerto No. 2:** Cellist Alban Gerhardt was hailed as a consummate artist. Reviewers noted his impeccable pitch, clean articulation, and the elegance of his classical phrasing. Rather than treating the Haydn concerto as a simple display of velocity, Gerhardt brought a warm, chamber-like intimacy to the *Adagio* that thoroughly captivated the hall.
*   **Mussorgsky’s Pictures at an Exhibition:** Moody’s reading of this showpiece was described as energetic and highly detailed. 
    *   The principal brass players (particularly trumpet and horn) were lauded for their flawless execution of Ravel's difficult writing, from the opening *Promenade* to the massive chords of *The Great Gate of Kiev*.
    *   Woodwind solos in *Il vecchio castello* and *Tuileries* were praised for their expressive, colorful character.
*   **The Verdict:** Critics noted that Robert Moody built an immediate and highly successful rapport with the MSO musicians. The performance was characterized by sharp rhythmic precision and a wide range of colors, making a strong case for Moody as a conductor of interest. The weekend concluded with an enthusiastic and warm reception from the Madison audience.
