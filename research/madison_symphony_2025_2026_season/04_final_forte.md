# 🎻 The Final Forte (2026 Bolz Young Artist Competition)

**Date:** March 4, 2026  
**Conductor:** John DeMain  
**Featured Finalists:**  
*   **Lorenz Fradkin-Annen** (Violin - Oregon High School)  
*   **Cordelia Momo Fredrickson** (Violin - Wisconsin Virtual Academy)  
*   **Zachary Lemke** (Marimba - Oregon High School)  
*   **André Peck** (Piano - Logan High School)  
**Venue:** Overture Hall, Madison, WI  

---

## 📖 Concert Background & Context
Held on Wednesday, March 4, 2026, *Wisconsin Young Artists Compete: The Final Forte* was the final round of the Madison Symphony Orchestra’s annual **Bolz Young Artist Competition**. The 2026 event marked the 20th anniversary of the statewide televised broadcast of the competition, which is produced in partnership with **PBS Wisconsin** and **Wisconsin Public Radio (WPR)**.

The competition is open to Wisconsin high school students (grades 9–12) who undergo rigorous preliminary and semi-final rounds. The four finalists perform as soloists with the full Madison Symphony Orchestra under the baton of John DeMain. The concert is broadcast live to a statewide audience. The first-place winner is awarded the **Marian Bolz Prize for Distinguished Musical Achievement** ($2,000 scholarship) and performs as a soloist the next day at the MSO's Spring Young People's Concert. The second-place winner receives the **Steenbock Youth Music Award** ($2,000 scholarship), and the remaining two finalists receive honorable mentions ($1,000 scholarships).

---

## 🎼 Repertoire & Soloists
The program featured the four young soloists performing single movements/selections from major concertos:

*   **Lorenz Fradkin-Annen** (Violin - Oregon High School): Performed the Cadenza and 4th movement of Dmitry Shostakovich’s *Violin Concerto No. 1 in A minor, Op. 77*.
*   **Cordelia Momo Fredrickson** (Violin - Wisconsin Virtual Academy): Performed the 1st movement of Serge Prokofiev’s *Violin Concerto No. 1 in D Major, Op. 19*.
*   **Zachary Lemke** (Marimba - Oregon High School): Performed Keiko Abe’s *Prism Rhapsody* for Marimba and Orchestra.
*   **André Peck** (Piano - Logan High School): Performed the 1st movement of Serge Prokofiev’s *Piano Concerto No. 1 in D-flat major, Op. 10* — **First Place Winner (Marian Bolz Prize)**.

## ✍️ Critical Review & Audience Reception
The Final Forte is widely celebrated in Wisconsin as one of the premier youth music events in the country. Post-event commentary and local broadcast reviews highlighted the following:

*   **High Technical Standard:** Commentators noted that all four finalists performed with a level of maturity and technical precision that rivaled professional soloists. 
    *   **Zachary Lemke** was highly praised for his fluid marimba technique and rhythmic energy, which brought a unique percussive texture to Overture Hall.
    *   The violinists, **Lorenz Fradkin-Annen** and **Cordelia Momo Fredrickson**, were commended for their secure intonation and expressive, singing tones.
    *   Pianist **André Peck** delivered a commanding, brilliant performance, demonstrating impressive keyboard control, rich tone, and deep musical maturity.
*   **Supportive Conducting:** Music Director John DeMain was praised for his sensitive, reassuring accompaniment. Conducting for young students in a high-pressure, televised competition requires careful cueing and balancing of the orchestra to ensure the soloists are never drowned out, which DeMain executed with masterclass precision.
*   **Broad Appeal:** The broadcast production by PBS Wisconsin and WPR was lauded for its high visual and audio quality, bringing classical music into homes across the state and celebrating the artistic achievements of Wisconsin's youth. The live audience in Overture Hall was highly supportive, greeting each student with enthusiastic applause.
*   **The Verdict:** Critics and observers agreed that the 20th anniversary of the broadcast was a fitting celebration during the MSO's centennial season, illustrating the orchestra’s ongoing dedication to educational outreach and community engagement.
