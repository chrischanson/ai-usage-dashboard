**Le Petit Yniold** → Mike S — a year ago

Struggled is not the right word. I'm with you on most of this. Meistersinger is a comedy. It ought to be funny and enjoyable and we should feel no guilt about laughing and having fun. So that was the good thing about yesterday. But there is a difference between taking comedy seriously and making the comedy serious. If one doesn't take it seriously, one will just play for laughs (the paté porcupine, the boxing ring, the stool. etc.) and that gets old quickly. If one does take it seriously, it can be just as profound an exploration of human experience as tragedy, but a lot more fun. It's easy to do and a mistake that many producers make is to assume that light means easy. A favorite example is the famous Giorgio Strehler Marriage of Figaro, which was one of the funniest things I have ever seen (until I found myself in tears) because the humor came from the behavior of characters, not gags. It was both hilarious and very real. As an actor on his deathbed is supposed to have said (but it has been attributed to so many that I doubt anyone actually said it) "dying is easy, comedy is hard."

**Le Petit Yniold** → Larry Petrovich — a year ago

It's not too unusual for singers to bring a lot of their characterizations with them to a new production. In many cases, they have sung the role in more than one production and refined their approach, whereas the director has is likely not to have done the opera before. Sometimes working with a new director results in new insights, but at other times, a singer is lucky to have enough experience in a role to be able to survive inept direction. Left to her own devices (and here we agree completely) I think it's very unlikely that Christin Nilsson would have suggested that she appear as a floral bouquet in the last scene. I also doubt that Zeppenfeld would have suggested that David break a stool in the fight scene so that Sachs could spend the first five minutes of the third act repairing it in order to set up a lame pratfall for Beckmesser. Please don't get me wrong. There was a lot to enjoy about yesterday's performance. Meistersinger IS a comedy. It should have plenty of laughs and they don't always come easy. But as much as I did enjoy it, I found myself liking the production less and less. Would I have booed, if I were there? No. It was neither bad enough nor good enough for that.
